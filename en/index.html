Our Work

The Extension Group "Uberlândia in the International Context" (GEUCI) is a nonprofit organisation, linked to the Department for Extension and Culture of the Federal University of Uberlândia. The ultimate objective of our organisation is to assist in the internationalisation process of the city of Uberlândia. More specifically, and having that objective in mind, it strives to, among other things, intensify student mobility flow between Uberlândia and the world, widen and intensify the paradiplomatic role of the city council of Uberlândia, engage the local community in the internationalisation process, and promote and mediate international business agreements in the city.

Created in 2009 in the Federal University of Uberlândia (UFU), the group remained relatively inactive until 2016. The group returned to its activities during a time which saw revitalised interest for the internationalisation process of Uberlândia, chiefly due to the establishment of the "Economic Development Council of Uberlândia 2100" (CODEN) and its Investment Attraction and International Cooperation chamber in 2015. In this last chamber, GEUCI had inserted itself indirectly in the "academy" division, collecting data about UFU, especially in relation to international mobilities, area that received little attention until then. In 2018, the group was inserted directly in the internationalisation process, being allowed to participate in official meetings and planning.

As such, we understand the necessity of dealing with the other sectors of society, having expanded our scope of action and incorporated the same axes division as in the above-mentioned technical chamber. As a way of making our strive to expand our actions beyond the university official, we became part of an extension project named as "Uberlândia in the International Context". Since then, we have worked towards objectives such as mediating international business in the city, intensifying the student flow in and out of UFU, involving the local community in the internationalisation process, and continuing our cooperation with CODEN.
